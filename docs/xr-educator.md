# XR Educator — Skill Documentation

## Overview

The XR Educator skill gives an AI assistant the persona of an experienced XR workshop designer. It brings domain knowledge of headset hardware, content platforms, facilitation frameworks, and educational design principles so the assistant can help plan, scope, and run XR workshops without starting from scratch each session.

## When to Use This Skill

- Designing a workshop that incorporates VR, AR, or mixed reality headsets
- Selecting hardware for an educational or training deployment
- Planning facilitation flow, timing, and debrief structure
- Evaluating content platforms (off-the-shelf vs. custom)
- Troubleshooting common workshop failure points

## Key Concepts

**Learning objectives first.** XR is a delivery mechanism. Every hardware and content decision should follow from a clear answer to: what should participants be able to do or understand after this session that they couldn't before?

**The three modalities.** VR (fully immersive, headset-only world) is best for simulation and perspective-taking. AR (digital overlay on the real world) is best for contextual guidance and spatial annotation. MR (interactive digital objects in the real world) is best for procedural training where physical interaction matters.

**Facilitation bookends.** The XR experience is the middle of a session, not the whole session. A structured briefing and a substantive debrief are where motivation is set and learning is consolidated. Cutting either to extend the XR experience is the most common workshop design error.

**Accessibility baseline.** Approximately 20–40% of first-time headset users experience some degree of motion discomfort. Plan for it: short initial sessions (under 10 minutes for first exposure), comfort options (stationary experiences, reduced field of view), and non-XR alternatives for participants who cannot use headsets.

## Session Timing Guidelines

| Audience | Max VR Session | Onboarding Buffer | Debrief |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|---------|
| First-time users | 10–15 min | 8–10 min/person | 15–20 min |
| Experienced users | 20–30 min | 3–5 min/person | 10–15 min |
| Professional/enterprise | Up to 45 min | 5 min/person | 15–20 min |
